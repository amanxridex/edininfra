RERA Explained

What is RERA?

RERA stands for the Real Estate Regulatory Authority, established under the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016. It is a government body designed to protect homebuyers and boost investments. As of 2026, RERA has evolved into "RERA 2.0" — featuring AI-powered digital monitoring and even stricter accountability for developers.

📋

Mandatory Registration

No developer can advertise or sell a property without first registering with their state's RERA authority. Verify any project's RERA Number on official state portals.

🏦

70% Escrow Rule

Builders must deposit 70% of funds collected into a dedicated escrow account for that specific construction — fully protecting your investment from day one.

📐

Carpet Area Standardisation

Builders must sell based on Carpet Area (actual usable floor space) — not the confusing "Super Built-up Area." Pay only for what you get.

🛠️

5-Year Defect Liability

If any structural defects arise within 5 years of possession, the builder is legally obligated to fix them for free. Zero cost to the buyer.

💰

Delay Compensation

If a project is delayed, the builder must pay monthly interest (SBI rate + 2%) for every month of the delay. Your time is legally valued.

🤖

RERA 2.0 — New in 2026

AI-driven progress tracking, 60-day digital dispute resolution, and mandatory third-party escrow audits make 2026 the most transparent year in Indian real estate history.
